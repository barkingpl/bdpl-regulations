## DEFINITIONS

**Registered Player:** A player who has completed registration, paid all fees, been added to their team's official roster, and has not been suspended or removed.

**Committee:** The elected body of 3-7 members responsible for administering the League, including a Chair and Vice Chair.

**Match Night:** The scheduled evening on which a league fixture or competition match takes place.

**Official Channels:** Divisional WhatsApp groups, League website, and any other communication platforms designated by the Committee.

**Spirit of the Game:** The principle that all participants will act honestly, fairly, and in accordance with both the letter and intent of these regulations.

# PART 1 — GOVERNANCE & AUTHORITY

## 1.1 League Identity

The League shall be known as the **Barking and District Pool League (BDPL)**.

## 1.2 Committee Composition & Role

### Composition
The Committee shall consist of **3-7 elected members**, including a Chair and Vice Chair. Committee members serve as stewards of the League and are expected to act impartially and in the best interests of all participants.

### Responsibilities
- Administering all League matters including fixtures, competitions, disputes, and communications
- Enforcing these regulations consistently and fairly
- Making determinations in ambiguous situations
- Closing loopholes and preventing abuse of regulations
- Managing League finances and membership

## 1.3 Committee Authority

### General Authority
- The Committee has **full and final authority** to administer the League and interpret these regulations
- The Committee's decisions on all League matters are **final and binding**
- **There is no appeals process**
- Where these regulations are silent on a matter, the Committee will make a ruling based on precedent, fairness, and the spirit of the game

### Regulatory Powers

The Committee may:
- Amend **Procedural Rules** (day-to-day operations) at any time without team approval
- Amend **Structural Rules** (league format, promotion/relegation, fundamental rights) only at AGM or EGM, unless emergency circumstances require immediate action
- **Close loopholes immediately** with new or amended regulations
- **Apply amended regulations retrospectively** to matches where the Committee determines a loophole was exploited knowingly or unfairly
- Impose sanctions on teams or individuals who exploited loopholes, even if they technically complied with previous wording

### Emergency Powers
In exceptional circumstances (including but not limited to public health emergencies, venue closures affecting multiple teams, or serious threats to League integrity), the Committee may suspend or modify any regulation temporarily, including Structural Rules, with immediate effect. Such emergency measures will be reviewed at the next general meeting.

### Communication & Notice
All Committee decisions, rule changes, and official notices will be communicated via the official channels.

## 1.4 Meetings

### Annual General Meeting (AGM)
- Held on the **2nd Tuesday of December** (or alternative date as determined by Committee with notice to teams)
- Start time: **20:15**
- Open to all registered players
- Teams failing to attend: **£10 fine**

### AGM Motions
- Must be submitted **in writing** to the Committee
- Deadline: **The delegates meeting one month before the AGM**
- Decisions by majority vote; Chair has casting vote if tied

### Extraordinary General Meeting (EGM)
- Proposals must be submitted in writing at least **one month and one week** in advance (two months where possible)
- Proposals presented to teams one month prior to vote
- Majority vote required; Chair has casting vote if tied
- Divisional WhatsApp groups are acceptable for written submissions

## 1.5 Delegates Meetings

### Frequency
Three delegates meetings will be held during the 2026 season.

### Attendance
Each team must send at least one representative to each meeting.

### Penalties for Non-Attendance

| Meeting Missed | Fine | Point Deduction |
|----------------|------|-----------------|
| 1st meeting | £25 per team | None |
| 2nd meeting | £25 per team | 1 league point |
| 3rd meeting | Committee discretion | Committee discretion |

- Penalties are **cumulative** (missing multiple meetings results in all penalties)
- Point deductions are from league points
- Fines must be paid within 14 days
- Teams should notify Committee in advance if unable to attend

# PART 2 — LEAGUE STRUCTURE

## 2.1 Season

The League season runs from **February to November**.

## 2.2 Divisions

- The League will operate with divisions as determined by the Committee
- Promotion and relegation between divisions will be determined by final league standings
- The Committee has discretion over divisional structure

## 2.3 Match Points & League Standings

### Points Awarded
- **Win:** 2 points
- **Draw:** 1 point
- **Loss:** 0 points

### Tiebreakers
If teams finish level on points:
1. **Frame difference** (frames won minus frames lost)
2. **Play-off match** using standard league format (4 singles, 2 doubles, 4 singles)

## 2.4 Match Format & Rules

### Standard Match Format
- **10 frames total:** 4 singles (S1-S4), 2 doubles (D1-D2), 4 singles (S5-S8)
- Played under **EPA/WPA rules** unless otherwise specified

### Break
- Home team breaks first in S1
- Break alternates thereafter

# PART 3 — MEMBERSHIP & REGISTRATION

## 3.1 Team Registration

- Teams must register before the season deadline set by the Committee
- Registration fee must be paid in full
- Teams must have a designated captain and home venue

## 3.2 Player Registration

- All players must be registered before playing any league match
- Registration fee: As set by the Committee
- Players may only be registered with **one team** at a time

### Registration Deadline
- Players must be registered **before** they play
- Playing an unregistered player: **£10 fine per player** + all frames involving that player awarded to opponents

## 3.3 Player Eligibility Rules

### Minimum Appearances
- Players must have played at least **FOUR (4) league matches** to enter most competitions
- See individual competition rules for specific requirements

### Playing for Multiple Teams
- Players registered with two teams simultaneously: **£50 fine**
- Player suspended; all frames count as losses
- May be expelled from the League

## 3.4 Team Withdrawals & Disbanding

- Teams withdrawing mid-season forfeit all remaining matches
- Results against withdrawn teams may be expunged at Committee discretion
- Registration fees are non-refundable

## 3.5 Fines & Financial Obligations

- All fines must be paid within **14 days** of issue
- Outstanding fines may result in points deductions or suspension
- See Part 7 for full fines schedule

# PART 4 — MATCH REGULATIONS

## 4.1 Team Composition & Line-ups

### Minimum Players
- Minimum **5 players** required to start a match
- Maximum **8 players** may be used

### Player Selection Rules
- Each player may play a **maximum of 2 singles frames**
- Any player playing two singles frames must play once in **S1-S4** and once in **S7-S8 ONLY** (NOT S5 or S6)
- Any player who plays two singles **cannot also play in doubles**
- P1 and P2 (doubles players) must be formed from players who only play one singles frame
- **Captain selects the team:** The captain has full authority over team selection

### Line-up Declaration
- Singles line-up (S1-S8) must be declared before the match
- Doubles pairings declared before doubles commence

## 4.2 Signing In & Team Sheet Deadlines

### Sign-in Deadline
- All players must be signed in by **20:30**
- Failure to have 5 players signed by deadline: **£10 fine** + frame forfeitures

### Late Arrivals
- Players arriving after 20:30 may only play frames not yet started
- They must be registered League players

## 4.3 Match Start

### Ready to Play
- Teams must be ready to start by **20:45**
- **Not ready by 20:45:** S1 & S2 forfeited (start 2-0 down) + **£10 fine**
- **Not ready by 21:00:** Entire match awarded 10-0 + **£10 fine**

## 4.4 Result Sheets & Match Recording

### Home Team Responsibility
It is the **HOME team's responsibility** to provide an official BDPL scorecard for every match. Home teams should maintain adequate supply and request more before running out.

### Point Deduction Penalty
If the home team cannot provide an official BDPL scorecard:
- **1 league point deduction** per occurrence
- Applies regardless of match result
- Result can still be submitted on paper, but point is lost

| Result | Normal Points | Without Scorecard |
|--------|---------------|-------------------|
| Win | 2 points | 1 point |
| Draw | 1 point | 0 points |
| Loss | 0 points | -1 point |

### Away Team Clarification
- Away teams are NOT expected to bring scorecards
- If away team uses unofficial cards when official was provided, standard penalties apply

### Emergency Waiver
The Committee may waive point deduction at its discretion if satisfied the situation was genuinely unavoidable.

### Result Submission
- Results must be submitted via photo to divisional WhatsApp by **Tuesday 23:59** following the match
- Late submission: **£10 fine**

### Incorrectly Filled Scorecards
- Player in wrong position (line-up breach): **£10 fine** + frames awarded to opponents
- Doubles pairing breach: **£10 fine** + affected doubles frames awarded to opponents

## 4.5 Referees

- Players referee each other's frames
- Either team may request an independent referee for any frame
- Referees should enforce EPA/WPA rules

## 4.6 Postponements & Rescheduling

- Postponements only granted at **Committee discretion**
- Must be requested as early as possible via official channels
- Rearranged matches must be played within timeframe set by Committee
- Failure to rearrange: Match may be awarded to non-offending team

## 4.7 Venues & Equipment Standards

- Home venues must have a regulation 7ft pool table
- Table and equipment must be in playable condition
- Venue must be available for the scheduled match time

## 4.8 Conduct & Behaviour

All members of the League must act at all times in a **friendly, sporting, honest and helpful way**.

### Unacceptable Conduct
- Verbal abuse, intimidation, or threatening behaviour
- Physical violence or threats of violence
- Deliberate rule manipulation or exploitation
- Bringing the League into disrepute

### Sanctions
The Committee may impose sanctions including:
- Fines
- Match bans
- Season bans
- Lifetime bans
- Expulsion from the League

## 4.9 Spirit of the Game

Teams and players are expected to honour both the **letter and spirit** of these regulations. Exploiting loopholes or technicalities contrary to the intent of the rules may result in sanctions.

## 4.10 Mobile Phones

Mobile phones should be on silent during frames. Use of phones during active play may be penalized.

## 4.11 Official Communication Channels

- **Divisional WhatsApp groups** are the primary communication channel
- All official notices will be posted here
- Teams are responsible for monitoring these channels

# PART 5 — LEAGUE COMPETITIONS

## 5.1 General Competition Rules

### Eligibility
To enter any League competition, a player must:
1. Be a currently registered League player in good standing
2. Have played at least **FOUR (4) league matches** during the current season
3. Have paid their competition entry fee by the deadline
4. Not be under suspension

### Finals Format
Unless otherwise specified, all individual competition finals are **Race to 4** (best of 7 frames). This is subject to change at the discretion of the Committee.

### Venue Selection
Competition venues selected by the Committee or by mutual agreement of participants.

## 5.2 Darren Henshaw KO Shield (Team Competition)

- **Format:** Standard league match format (4 singles, 2 doubles, 4 singles = 10 frames)
- **Tiebreaker:** One additional frame if 5-5
- **Final:** Played on Finals Night

## 5.3 Alan Pointer Trophy (Player of the Year)

- **Eligibility:** Each team may submit one player — the highest individual points-scoring **available** player at the end of the season
- **Divisional finals:** Race to 4
- **Overall final:** Race to 4

## 5.4 Jeff Fuller KO Singles

- **Eligibility:** Open to all registered players meeting general eligibility
- **Semi-finals:** Race to 4
- **Final:** Race to 4

## 5.5 Henry Thorpe KO Pairs

- **Eligibility:** Partners must BOTH be registered to the SAME team
- **All rounds:** Best of 3 frames
- **Final:** Race to 4

## 5.6 Pat Little Delegates Cup

- **Eligibility:** One nominated player per team who has EITHER:
  - Acted as team representative in official League business at least FOUR times during the season, OR
  - Is serving as a Committee member
- **Restriction:** No player may compete in BOTH this and the Tom McTaggert Memorial Trophy in the same year
- **Format:** Set annually by Committee based on entries
- **Final:** Race to 4

## 5.7 Tom McTaggert Memorial Trophy

- **Eligibility:** Each team may nominate ONE player
- **Recommendation:** Nominate the runner-up from the team's Player of the Year competition
- **Restriction:** No player may compete in BOTH this and the Pat Little Delegates Cup in the same year
- **Final:** Race to 4, played on Finals Night

## 5.8 Seniors KO Cup

- **Eligibility:** Players meeting age requirement as set by Committee
- **Final:** Race to 4

## 5.9 Ladies KO Cup

- **Eligibility:** Female registered players
- **Final:** Race to 4

## 5.10 Young Player KO Cup

- **Eligibility:** Players meeting age requirement as set by Committee
- **Final:** Race to 4

## 5.11 Divisional Team Cups

- **Format:** Standard league match format
- **Final format:** As per Darren Henshaw Shield

## 5.12 Returnable Trophies

- Winners retain returnable trophies for one year
- Trophies must be returned in good condition before the following Finals Night
- Failure to return: Responsible party liable for replacement cost
- Committee maintains trophy log on website

# PART 6 — GRIEVANCES & DISPUTES

## 6.1 Who May Raise a Grievance

Any registered player or team captain may raise a grievance regarding:
- Match conduct or results
- Rule interpretations
- Player eligibility
- Other League matters

## 6.2 Match Result Confirmation

Match results become final when:
- Both captains have signed the result sheet, AND
- No grievance is raised within 48 hours

## 6.3 Initial Resolution

Grievances should first be raised in the **Divisional WhatsApp group** for transparency.

## 6.4 Divisional Support Review

If unresolved, the relevant Division Support person on the Committee will review the matter and attempt resolution with both Team Captains.

## 6.5 Full Committee Review

If still unresolved, the Division Support person presents all findings to the full Committee.

## 6.6 Conflict of Interest

If a Committee member has a conflicting interest, a panel shall be declared.

## 6.7 Decision

A majority decision will be sought. In the event of no majority, the League Chair (or Panel Chair) has the casting vote.

## 6.8 Finality

Committee decisions on grievances are **final and binding**. There is no appeals process.

# PART 7 — FINES & SANCTIONS

## 7.1 Financial Penalties

### Player Eligibility Violations

| Offence | Fine | Additional Penalty |
|---------|------|-------------------|
| Playing unregistered player | £10 per player | Frames awarded to opponents |
| Playing suspended player | £50 | Frames awarded to opponents + further sanctions |
| Player registered with two teams | £50 | Player suspended; frames count as losses |

### Match Start & Timing Violations

| Offence | Fine | Additional Penalty |
|---------|------|-------------------|
| Fewer than 5 players signed by 20:30 | £10 | Frames forfeited |
| Not ready by 20:45 | £10 | S1 & S2 forfeited |
| Not ready by 21:00 | £10 | Match awarded 10-0 |
| Line-up/signing breach | £10 | Affected frames awarded to opponents |
| Doubles declaration breach | £10 | Affected doubles frames awarded to opponents |

### Result Submission

| Offence | Fine |
|---------|------|
| Late result submission (after Tuesday 23:59) | £10 |
| Using unofficial scorecard (away team when official provided) | Warning, then £10 |

### Meeting Attendance

| Offence | Fine | Additional Penalty |
|---------|------|-------------------|
| Missing 1st delegates meeting | £25 | None |
| Missing 2nd delegates meeting | £25 | 1 league point deduction |
| Missing 3rd delegates meeting | Committee discretion | Committee discretion |
| Missing AGM | £10 | None |

## 7.2 Non-Financial Sanctions

### Point Deductions

| Offence | Deduction |
|---------|-----------|
| Home team cannot provide official scorecard | 1 league point per occurrence |
| Missing 2nd consecutive delegates meeting | 1 league point |

### Frame Forfeitures

| Situation | Frames Forfeited |
|-----------|------------------|
| Not ready by 20:45 | S1 & S2 |
| Not ready by 21:00 | Entire match (10-0) |
| Unregistered player | All frames involving that player |
| Line-up breach | Affected frames |

## 7.3 Fine Payment & Enforcement

- All fines must be paid within **14 days** of issue
- Outstanding fines may result in:
  - Points deductions
  - Suspension from competitions
  - Team suspension from league

## 7.4 Sanctions for Serious Incidents

The Committee may impose additional sanctions for serious misconduct:
- Extended match bans
- Season bans
- Lifetime bans
- Expulsion from the League

# PART 8 — MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS

## 8.1 Regulation Amendments

These regulations may be amended by:
- **Procedural Rules:** Committee at any time
- **Structural Rules:** AGM or EGM vote, or Committee in emergency circumstances

## 8.2 Interpretation

Where these regulations are ambiguous or silent, the Committee will interpret in accordance with:
1. The spirit of the game
2. Precedent from previous decisions
3. Fairness to all parties

## 8.3 Severability

If any provision of these regulations is found to be invalid, the remaining provisions continue in full force.

## 8.4 Contact

For queries about these regulations, contact the Committee via official channels.
